Prof. M.S. Satsangi

B. Arch. (Hons.), F.I.I.A., MCA
Principal Architect

About Prof. M.S. Satsangi

Professional Qualification

  • 1964
  • Bachelor of Architecture
  • Ist Class Honours
  • Indian Institute of Technology, Kharagpur, India

Professional Memberships

  • Registered Architect, Council of Architecture, Govt. of India
  • Fellow Member, Indian Institute of Architects

Early Years (1964 –1977)

  • After Graduation, abriefstint as Architect with Ballardie Thompson & Matthewsat Calcutta and a Senior Research Fellow at CBRI Roorkee, began the early career as a Post-graduate Trainee at National Institute of Design, Ahmedabad.
  • At NID,it was a good mix of professional experience, academic enhancement in all related design disciplines, along with exposure to Internationally known Designers and Engineers namely Charles Eames, FreiOtto, George Nakashima and many more, as well as abeginningofa teaching career.
  • While learning professional photography, also taught Graphic Presentation & Perspective courses to Product Design students of NID.
  • Working on Institute’s projects with Internationally known Architectsand Teachers Prof. Harry Weese & Prof. Louis I. Kahn, completed Air India Housing at Bombay and Indian Institute of Management at Ahmedabad, working both in Chicago and Philadelphia, U.S.A & Ahmedabad and Bombay, India while participating in the establishment and growth of the National Institute of Design, Ahmedabad, itself.
  • First Full-time Teaching assignment was as an Assistant Professor (1972)in the School of Architecture at CEPT, Ahmedabad, teaching and working along with stalwarts like Ar. B. V. Doshi and Ar. Anant Raje.
  • Established the School’s Workshop and built several full-scale projects with students such as Geodesic Domes, Tensigriti Masts,Space frames, Grid-shells & Light- weight Fabric Structures. Developed good understanding of the Form-Finding Processes through joint teaching with Eda Schaur,an associate of Prof. FreiOttoof Germany.

(1977 –1985)

  • Period of professional work in Nigeria startedas Project Architect(1977–1979), Chief Architect (1979 – 1981) and as Associate of the Firm Frank Mbanefo & Associates(1981–1985).
  • Wide range of Projects included Residential, Commercial, Institutional, Industrial and Religious Projects. Some of theLand mark Projects include Central Bank of Nigeria Staff- Housingat Enuguandat Calabar Nigeria,New Government HouseComplex at Bauchi,Nigeria,FederalPolytechnic Building atIdah,Nigeria,Vice-Presidential Complex at Federal Capital, Abuja, Nigeria,United Bank for Africa Limited at Enugu and also at Port Harcourt, Nigeria, Central Spare parts Depot of Anambra Motor Manufacturing Company at Enugu,Nigeria and Mosque at University of Nigeria at Nnsuka, Nigeria.

(1985 –2000)

  • After return from Nigeria and settling downin New Delhi,this period was dominated primarily in teaching and simultaneous professional practice.
  • Initially started teaching asvisiting professor at School of Planning and Architecture, New Delhi,one stablishment of Sushant School of Art & Architecture in Gurgaon,took up to full- time teaching, design and construction and development of the academic programmes of the School.
  • Along with establishing the School’s Workshop for learning by doing things by hand, created awareness about Light weight Structures and constructed several full-scale structures with the help of students.
  • Conducted several workshops to develop under standing of Constructive Geometries & Light weight structures which included construction of full-scale structures like Domes, Grid Shells,Tensigriti Structures, Tensile Fabric Structures in several schools of architecture around the country. Vallabh Vidya Nagar, Kolhapur, Hamirpur and CEPT, Ahmedabad to name a few.
  • Teaching at all levels and conducting studios for Housing, Urban design, and guiding several Dissertations and final year Design Thesis, retiredas Professor and Head of the Department of Architecture from Sushant School in 2000.
  • Apart from teaching, this period also witness edintense professional activity that included participation in several competition projects: Naval Academy at Ezimala, Andhra Pradesh Open University at Hyderabad,Indira Gandhi National Open University at New Delhi, along with winning projects at Residential and Commercial Complex at Gandhi nagar,and at Hari Teerath, bothat Varanasi for Varanasi Development Authority to name a few.
  • Worked in association with Ar.A P Kanvinde for Indian Institute of Architects, New Delhi and Prof.Gopal Mitra of Calcutta on Modern Academy School Building at Kosi- Kalan, Residential Complex at Neelanchal, Calcutta, and Office Complex at Woods Street, Calcutta.
  • Asan independent practice worked on several Residential, Housing, Institutional and Industrial Projects.
